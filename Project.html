The Food Pyramid

The food pyramid is a visual representation of the optimal number of servings of food a person should eat daily from each basic food group. Many countries across the globe have adapted versions of the food pyramid, sometimes discarding the pyramid shape altogether. Whatever form they take, such food guides are intended to help people cultivate a daily pattern of recommended (and thus presumably healthy) food choices.

1. Base layer (Grain)

Whole grains:

Bread, rice, pasta, and cereal.

Recommendations:

6-11 servings per day.

Note:

Choose whole grains over refined grains for better nutrition and fiber.

2. Second Layer (Fruits and Vegetables)

Fruit

Vegtables

Recommendations:

3-5 servings of vegetables and 2-4 servings of fruits per day

Note:

Aim for a variety of colors to get a range of nutrients.

3. Third Layer (Proteins and Dairy)

Diary

Protein

Recommendations:

2-3 servings of proteins and 2-3 servings of dairy per day.

Note:

Opt for lean proteins and low-fat or fat-free dairy options.

4. Top Layer (Fats, Oils, and Sweets)

Sweets

Fats & Oils

Recommendations:

Use sparingly.

Note:

Choose healthy fats like olive oil and limit sugar intake.
